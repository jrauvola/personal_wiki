## Empirical state 2026-04-23

Phase 1 eval + the F1-F6 inert-latent test battery (`research_findings/inert_latent_hypothesis_tests.md`, `research_findings/f1_f2_f3_inert_latent_tests.md`) have converged on a concrete mechanistic picture of what CODI-at-4B actually learns. All numbers below are V2 bf16 on Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 at `num_latent=8` unless noted.

**Capability collapse (Phase 1).** Zero-shot Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 scores 0.86 on GSM8k; V2 bf16 scores 0.16 — CODI training collapses roughly 70 percentage points of base capability. The Track A first-sentence-trim regrade flattens the rising `num_latent` accuracy curve, showing the "more latents → more accuracy" signal was a loop-lucky-match artifact, not iterative computation ([[Shortcut Behavior]]).

**F1-F6 synthesis.** Strict "latents are fully inert" is refuted — F4 ablation costs 25-29% relative accuracy and F5's proxy shows non-trivial per-example KV variation (median pairwise cosine 0.78, 63 PCs for 95% variance). But the weaker form holds: CODI-at-4B's latents are *functionally inert for reasoning but structurally necessary for template routing*. F1 finds only 28 unique-correct predictions across all three benchmarks (2.8% of V2-correct). F2 shows loops are context-free (12% question-digit overlap). F3 shows 7/8 latent positions decode to a fixed template (`The → 0 → 0 → ? → . → . → . → .`). F5 direct shows cross-example KV swap leaves B's accuracy at 0.100 → 0.100 and changes only 13% of predictions. F6 shows σ=0.5 noise collapses accuracy to <3% — a narrow geometric basin required for routing.

**What this means for the north-star.** The latents function as a geometric key the decoder uses to lock into its `"The answer is: ..."` format-prior attractor — a case of [[Feature Collapse]] expressed through a fixed-template routing key rather than through hidden-state uniformity. Any workable latent-reasoning model built by synthesizing V2 / [[SIM-CoT]] / [[Latent Thoughts Tuning]] must break this template-routing mode before scaling. [[Context-Prediction-Fusion]] (LT-Tuning and the 5-way CPF convergence) and aux-decoder supervision (SIM-CoT) are the two mechanisms currently hypothesized to force latents to carry step-identifiable content rather than acting as a template key. See [[state-of-project-2026-04-23]] for the full snapshot.

## Post-2026-04-23 update addendum (2026-04-24 late)

- **Branch 1 layer-asymmetric probe — CONFIRM** (`research_findings/layer_probe/v2_bf16_step3/layer_asymmetric_probe_v2_bf16.md`, 500 GSM8k × 37 layers, V2 bf16 step 3). F3's template-lock is a **final-layer readout artifact**, not a stack-wide property. Per-layer geometric dispersion traces a clean U: rises from embed through mid-stack peak L22-L30 (median pair cos 0.966 at L28, top-1 PC variance 0.137 at L30, 95%-PC count 213 at L22), then **re-collapses** sharply across L31-L36 (final L36: pair cos 0.989, top-1 PC 0.310 — 2.27× the trough; 95%-PC count 82 — 38% of the mid-stack peak). Mid-stack carries per-example reasoning content; the last 6 layers compress that content into the routing template before it is written to decoder-facing KV cache. Mechanistically explains F5's swap-null (swapping final-layer KV swaps two copies of the template). Direct V3 implication: middle-layer [[Context-Prediction-Fusion]] is on the table — anchor `h_{t-1, L≈28-30}` instead of final-layer hidden. Logit-lens-per-layer deferred (Qwen3-4B shard 1/3 not locally cached). Resolves the 2026-04-23 F3 layer-wise deferred item.

## Open questions

- Can V2 (detach) + [[SIM-CoT]] (auxiliary decoder) + [[Latent Thoughts Tuning]] (CPF) be composed coherently, or do they overlap?
- Curriculum/alignment necessity — theorem says yes, empirics say no (4-way cluster). Resolution → writeup methodology.
- Retrofit existing LMs to recurrent-depth ([[Retrofitted Recurrence]] / [[Encode Think Decode]]) vs post-hoc latent scaffolding (CODI / LT-Tuning) — which first on the north-star?
- Is the [[Context-Prediction-Fusion]] convergence (5 independent groups) evidence of a fundamental inductive bias, or convergent engineering to the same local optimum?
- **New (post F1-F6):** Does Phase 2 LT-Tuning CPF break the template-routing lock observed in V2? I.e. does F3 entropy rise above 0.4 bits on the non-step-3 positions, and does F5 swap move B's accuracy by more than a few percent? (Phase 2 LT-Tuning training is in flight — this is the first clean test.)
- **New (post F1-F6):** Is the narrow geometric basin / template-routing failure mode universal at 4B, or specific to Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507? Re-running F3/F4/F5/F6 on any Phase 2 checkpoint that achieves >30% GSM8k accuracy is the planned discriminator.
- **New (post F1-F6):** Is the F1 "no unique reasoning capability beyond base model" result an artifact of CODI's aggressive capability collapse, or a universal property of post-hoc latent scaffolding on already-strong base models? Discriminator: run F1 on a SIM-CoT or LT-Tuning checkpoint on the same base model.
- **Retained:** Which interpretability methodology to lead with — backtracking + forward-chaining ([[Are LRMs Easily Interpretable]]), do-interventions ([[Dynamics of Latent CoT]]), tuned lens, process-reward-classifier ([[Latent Thinking Optimization]]), or geometric signatures ([[Two-Scale Latent Dynamics]])? F1-F6 uses logit-lens + KV ablation / swap / noise — the F5 "KV swap" intervention is effectively a do-intervention in the [[Dynamics of Latent CoT]] style and generalizes to Phase 2 checkpoints.
